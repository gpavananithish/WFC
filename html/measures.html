Safety & Precautions

Comprehensive weather safety protocols designed to protect you, your family, and your livelihood in every climate condition.

Critical Precautions for Every Condition

Sunny & UV High

  • Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen every 2 hours.
  • Wear UV-rated sunglasses and hats.
  • Limit outdoor activity from 11 AM - 4 PM.
  • Wear light-colored, loose-fitting clothing.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid sugary drinks.

Rainy & Monsoon

  • Carry high-quality waterproof gear.
  • Avoid standing under trees during lightning.
  • Drive slowly to prevent hydroplaning.
  • Stay away from power lines and water bodies.
  • Unplug electronic appliances during storms.

Snow & Extreme Cold

  • Dress in multiple layers of warm clothing.
  • Check for signs of frostbite and hypothermia.
  • Keep heating systems properly maintained.
  • Use insulated boots and hand warmers.
  • Avoid continuous outdoor exposure.

Blizzard & Travel

  • Avoid non-essential travel during blizzards.
  • Keep an emergency kit in your vehicle.
  • Clear snow from exhaust vents for safety.
  • Keep a backup shovel and sand for traction.
  • Share your travel route with family.

Storm & High Winds

  • Secure loose outdoor objects like furniture.
  • Stay away from windows and glass doors.
  • Keep power banks charged for outages.
  • Stay indoors until the storm subsides.
  • Watch for flying debris and broken branches.

Excessive Heat

  • Drink water every 15 minutes, even if not thirsty.
  • Never leave children or pets in a vehicle.
  • Use fans or A/C to prevent heatstroke.
  • Eat small, light meals to stay energized.
  • Take cold showers to lower body temp.

Safety Protocols for Vulnerable Groups

Children

Supervise outdoor activities closely. Ensure they are drinking fluids every 30 minutes during sunny days.

Elderly

Regular check-ins are vital during extreme humidity or cold. Maintain temperate indoor environments.

Pets

Provide double the water during heat spells. Check pavement temperature with your hand before walking them.

Commuters

Check wipers and tire pressure before long trips. Carry emergency blankets and a flashlight during winter storms.
